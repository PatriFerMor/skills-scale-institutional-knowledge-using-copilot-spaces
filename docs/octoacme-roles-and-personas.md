# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Key Interactions
- **With UX Designers**: Receive design specifications and collaborate on implementation feasibility
- **With QA Engineers**: Coordinate on test coverage, defect fixes, and acceptance criteria verification
- **With DevOps**: Work together on CI/CD integration and deployment readiness
- **With Product Managers**: Implement prioritized backlog items and provide technical estimates
- **With Project Managers**: Provide daily status updates and escalate blockers

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed
- Defect reports and test feedback

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Key Interactions
- **With Developers**: Clarify requirements, define acceptance criteria, and provide feedback on implementations
- **With UX Designers**: Collaborate on user research, feature prioritization, and usability validation
- **With QA Engineers**: Define quality expectations and acceptance criteria
- **With Business Analysts**: Align on requirements and business context
- **With Support/Customer Success**: Gather user feedback and validate product-market fit
- **With Project Managers**: Align on roadmap priorities and release planning

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs
- Metrics reviews and impact analysis

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Key Interactions
- **With Developers**: Track progress, identify blockers, and escalate risks
- **With Product Managers**: Align on scope, priorities, and milestone planning
- **With QA Engineers**: Coordinate testing timelines and release readiness
- **With DevOps**: Confirm deployment readiness and coordinate release windows
- **With Business Analysts**: Clarify scope and requirements changes
- **With Support/Customer Success**: Coordinate rollout plans and stakeholder communications

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation
- Escalation paths and blocker resolution

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers ensure usability, accessibility, and design consistency throughout the project. They collaborate with Product Managers and Developers to translate requirements into intuitive user flows and interfaces.

### Responsibilities
- Create user flows, wireframes, and design specifications
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Ensure accessibility standards and design consistency
- Provide design feedback and validation during development
- Collaborate on design system maintenance and evolution

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, accessible user experiences
- Reduce user friction and increase adoption
- Maintain design consistency across products and features

### Key Interactions
- **With Product Managers**: Validate user research, inform prioritization, and define success metrics
- **With Developers**: Provide detailed design specifications, review implementations, and collaborate on feasibility
- **With QA Engineers**: Define usability acceptance criteria and participate in user acceptance testing
- **With Business Analysts**: Understand business requirements and user context
- **With Support/Customer Success**: Gather user feedback and identify design improvement opportunities

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and feedback sessions
- User research findings and insights
- Design specifications and component libraries
- Usability testing results and recommendations

---

## QA Engineer

### Role Summary
QA Engineers own the creation and execution of test plans and ensure quality standards are met prior to release. They verify features against acceptance criteria and coordinate manual and automated testing efforts.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain test plans aligned with acceptance criteria
- Design and execute manual and automated test cases
- Identify, document, and track defects
- Verify fixes and conduct regression testing
- Coordinate quality assurance across development phases
- Provide quality metrics and risk assessment

### Goals
- Ensure features meet acceptance criteria and quality standards
- Reduce defects and regressions in production
- Enable confident, timely releases

### Key Interactions
- **With Developers**: Communicate defects, testing needs, and signoff requirements
- **With Product Managers**: Define quality expectations and acceptance criteria
- **With UX Designers**: Validate usability acceptance criteria and user acceptance testing
- **With Project Managers**: Report testing status, timelines, and release readiness
- **With Support/Customer Success**: Identify edge cases and coordinate post-release verification

### Typical Communication
- Test plans and test case documentation
- Defect reports and status updates
- Quality metrics and risk assessments
- Release readiness reports and sign-off

---

## DevOps

### Role Summary
DevOps Engineers design and maintain CI/CD pipelines, infrastructure, and deployment automation. They work closely with Developers to ensure smooth code delivery and with Project Managers to communicate readiness for releases and incident management.

### Responsibilities
- Design and maintain CI/CD pipelines and deployment automation
- Manage infrastructure, environments, and monitoring
- Ensure security, reliability, and performance of systems
- Coordinate deployments and rollback procedures
- Provide incident response and post-incident analysis

### Goals
- Enable fast, reliable, and safe deployments
- Minimize downtime and deployment risks
- Maintain secure, observable infrastructure

### Key Interactions
- **With Developers**: Support CI/CD integration, provide deployment feedback, and assist with troubleshooting
- **With Project Managers**: Communicate deployment readiness, coordinate release windows, and report on deployment metrics
- **With QA Engineers**: Provide test environments and coordinate smoke testing
- **With Support/Customer Success**: Respond to incidents and coordinate incident communications

### Typical Communication
- Deployment schedules and readiness confirmations
- Infrastructure and pipeline documentation
- Incident reports and post-mortems
- Metrics and performance dashboards

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts bridge the gap between business requirements and technical implementation. They clarify needs, ensure alignment, and facilitate improvements in business processes and project workflows.

### Responsibilities
- Gather and document business requirements
- Clarify scope, success criteria, and trade-offs with stakeholders
- Facilitate requirements workshops and stakeholder interviews
- Identify process improvements and optimization opportunities
- Validate that implemented solutions meet business objectives

### Goals
- Ensure clear alignment between business needs and technical delivery
- Reduce scope ambiguity and rework
- Identify opportunities for process improvement and efficiency

### Key Interactions
- **With Product Managers**: Align on business context, requirements prioritization, and success metrics
- **With Developers**: Clarify requirements, provide business context, and validate technical feasibility
- **With Project Managers**: Define scope, timeline, and resource needs
- **With UX Designers**: Understand user context and business process flows
- **With Stakeholders**: Gather requirements and validate acceptance of deliverables

### Typical Communication
- Requirements documentation and specifications
- Stakeholder interview notes and workshop summaries
- Process maps and workflow diagrams
- Scope clarification and change requests

---

## Support / Customer Success

### Role Summary
Support and Customer Success teams provide feedback from end-users, assist in feature rollouts, and ensure post-release adoption and satisfaction. They are the voice of the customer and advocate for improvements based on user feedback.

### Responsibilities
- Gather and communicate user feedback and requests
- Assist with feature rollouts and user adoption
- Monitor user satisfaction and product usage metrics
- Identify training, documentation, or process improvements
- Escalate critical user-impacting issues and coordinate resolutions

### Goals
- Maximize user adoption and satisfaction
- Identify product improvements based on real-world usage
- Reduce support burden through better product design

### Key Interactions
- **With Product Managers**: Provide user feedback, validate feature requests, and communicate impact metrics
- **With Developers**: Escalate critical user issues and provide context for bug fixes
- **With UX Designers**: Identify usability issues and user pain points
- **With Project Managers**: Coordinate rollout plans, training, and stakeholder communications
- **With QA Engineers**: Validate fixes in production and report post-release issues

### Typical Communication
- User feedback summaries and feature request tracking
- Adoption metrics and user satisfaction surveys
- Support tickets and escalation reports
- Training materials and documentation feedback
