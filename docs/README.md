## Overview: OctoAcme's Approach to Project Management

OctoAcme follows a structured, lifecycle-based approach to project management that prioritizes customer value, iterative delivery, and clear ownership. The organization operates through five primary phases:

### The Five Phases

1. **Initiation** — Validate business need, identify stakeholders, and establish success metrics through a Project One-pager. This phase culminates in a go/no-go decision to move into planning.

2. **Planning** — Break work into prioritized, estimated backlog items with clear acceptance criteria and dependencies. Define the Definition of Done, release plan, and milestone map to create a shared understanding of what success looks like.

3. **Execution & Tracking** — Deliver incrementally through regular standups, sprints, and reviews. Enforce quality through testing, CI/CD, and code review. Escalate blockers and risks through a three-level system for rapid resolution.

4. **Release & Deployment** — Standardize how features reach production with pre-release checklists, smoke tests, rollback plans, and post-deploy verification to minimize risk and maintain observability.

5. **Retrospective & Continuous Improvement** — Capture learnings after sprints and releases. Convert insights into prioritized action items with clear owners and timelines to drive ongoing improvement.

### Key Themes Across All Phases

- **Clear Ownership**: Each project has a named Project Manager (PM) and Product Manager (PdM) who coordinate delivery and define outcomes.
- **Regular Cadence**: Daily standups (15 min), weekly delivery syncs, weekly PM/PdM alignment, and monthly stakeholder updates ensure consistent communication.
- **Quality First**: Unit tests, integration tests, end-to-end smoke tests, security scanning, and manual QA are built into every delivery cycle.
- **Risk Visibility**: A maintained risk register, escalation paths, and weekly reviews keep risks transparent and actionable.
- **Data-Informed**: Success metrics are identified early, tracked throughout execution, and used to measure impact and guide decisions.

### Core Roles

- **Project Managers** coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications.
- **Product Managers** define what should be built, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.
- **Developers** implement features, write tests, and collaborate on design and quality.
- **QA/Testing** validate quality and acceptance criteria.
- **Stakeholders** provide inputs, feedback, and approvals.

## Key Artifacts You'll Create

Across the OctoAcme lifecycle, you'll maintain these core artifacts:

- **Project One-pager** — Problem, goal, success metrics, stakeholders, timeline, risks, and proposed team.
- **Project Charter** — Comprehensive plan with scope, resources, milestones, and dependencies.
- **Prioritized Backlog** — Items with acceptance criteria, estimates, and owners.
- **Risk Register** — ID, description, impact, likelihood, mitigation, and status.
- **Sprint/Iteration Plans** — Pull and commit to items that meet DoD and have clear criteria.
- **Release Notes** — Summary of changes, migration steps, and known issues.
- **Retrospective Notes** — What went well, improvements, and action items with owners and due dates.
